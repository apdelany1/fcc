Cheesecake Introduction

Cheesecake began many many years ago with the first dairy farmer. He wanted some dessert in his stone oven that was delicious and would kill him quickly. No better way to commit suicide by cake than by eating a cheeseckae with cherry toppings. They didnt even have toppings back then; or crust. It was jsut cheese, sugar, eggs, and some lemon. Those were the days

Cheesecakes have evolved much since Johny Cheesecakes built the first Cheesecake in Nova Scotia. We make them different but the intent is still the same. There are 3 reasons we make and eat cheesecakes

  • We want to eat the BEST dessert ever known to mankind, just to say we did it
  • We want to be able to taste heaven
  • We want to eat a dessert that can kill us

My Cheesecake story

I started making cheesecakes back in the early days of the pandemic (for you kids who dont know what the pandemic is, it was pretty much a year long 'time-out'. It was bogus. Everyone was baking bread for some reason. I couldnt get yeast because every store sold out of yeast and flower. Why were we all making bread? Anyway, bread was horrible. I changed my tune up so I started making dessert. I always loved cheesecake. It seemed like a rich persons food. Nobody eats cheesecake when times are bad. Thats a "Times are good and everyones happy" type of desser. It was a big fail. Cheesecake is HARD. I made this website to teach you the skills necsesary to kill the cheesecake game and eliminate errors. Although, errors is good. It teaches you. No better way to learn than to fail hard.

The Perfect Cheesecake

The perfect cheesecake should not crumble. Lets just start there. Its the only cake that doesnt crumble. Thats just how cheesecake is meant to be. It is technically not even a cake! It is a custard but who cares about labels. The best cheesecake should be similar to Cheesecake Factory. They have a really good technique for making the cake nice and creamy. If you ask a cheesecake pro like myself what a good cheesecake shoud be like i tell them three things.

  • Thick
  • Creamy
  • Sweet

Before Beginning

Before you start. I want you to take out the blocks of cream cheese and let them sit a coupla hours. I want them to be nice and room temp before mixing. While the cream cheese sits. Have everything you need on hand. You dont want to be half way making the batter before you realize you dont have any sugar. Madone! Youll want to make sure you have space in the fridge clear and maybe a oven thermometor. You want to just have the next 2 hours be smooth af. No people in the way. Have a nice space set up in the fridge where youll put it after. Just be prepared for all.

Mise En Place

Mise En Place is something we all instictually do as humans. When we take a shower, we have the soap in a quick reach. We have the conditioner close, sponge is on your hip. Shampoo is ready for the quick draw. This is mise en place. Having everything quick at your reach. Its such a genius concept that when i cook now, i make sure i have all of the ingredients cut up and placed in a small bowl. So when i do stir fry, i just have to move my hand 12 inches to grab the bowl and pour it in the wok. This concept applies to baking obviously. Have ALL of your ingredients ready in small bowls. You will want them all out of the cabinet and measured so when it comes to add the sugar, BOOM its right there. Adding sour cream is no problem because its already out and measrued. Have everything ready for a quick draw. like a cheesecake deul

Crust

crust shall be backed first. This will go down while you are mixing the batter. Remember to make it nice and buttery in the pan. That will ensure a smooth removal. I will tell you this, the very first thing you should do is mix the crust ingredients with a food processor
